Double Materiality Assessment

The cornerstone of the Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD) that helps organizations identify and report on sustainability matters that truly impact their business and stakeholders.

What is Double Materiality?

Double materiality is a concept introduced by the EU's Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD) that requires companies to assess and report on sustainability matters from two perspectives:

Impact Materiality

How the company's activities impact the environment and society, including human rights.

Financial Materiality

How sustainability matters affect the company's development, performance, and position.

This dual perspective ensures that companies consider both their outward impact on society and environment, and the inward impact of sustainability issues on their business value.

The Double Materiality Assessment Process

Conducting a double materiality assessment involves a structured approach to identify and prioritize sustainability matters. Follow these steps to ensure CSRD compliance:

1

Define Assessment Scope

Establish the boundaries of your assessment, considering:

  • Your organization's value chain
  • Geographical locations of operations
  • Relevant business units and subsidiaries
  • Timeframe for the assessment
2

Identify Sustainability Matters

Compile a comprehensive list of potential sustainability matters using:

  • ESRS topical standards
  • Sector-specific guidelines
  • Global sustainability frameworks
  • Stakeholder concerns and expectations
3

Assess Impact Materiality

Evaluate each matter based on:

  • Scale of impact (severity)
  • Scope of impact (breadth)
  • Irremediable character
  • Likelihood of impact occurring
4

Assess Financial Materiality

Evaluate each matter based on:

  • Potential financial effects
  • Time horizon (short, medium, long-term)
  • Probability of occurrence
  • Connection to business model and strategy
5

Prioritize Material Matters

Combine both assessments to identify:

  • Matters material from both perspectives
  • Matters material only from one perspective
  • Materiality thresholds for reporting
  • Interconnections between matters
6

Report & Disclose

Integrate material matters into your reporting:

  • Prepare disclosures according to ESRS
  • Explain materiality assessment process
  • Present materiality matrix
  • Connect to business strategy

Best Practices for CSRD Compliance

Implementing an effective double materiality assessment requires careful planning and execution:

Stakeholder Engagement

Involve diverse stakeholders including investors, employees, communities, and suppliers throughout the process.

Dynamic Process

Treat materiality assessment as an ongoing process rather than a one-time exercise, with regular reviews.

Value Chain Focus

Extend your assessment beyond direct operations to include upstream and downstream value chain impacts.

Data-Driven Approach

Collect and analyze quantitative data alongside qualitative insights to support your materiality determinations.

Board Accountability

Ensure board-level oversight and approval of materiality assessment results and disclosures.

Audit Trail

Maintain comprehensive documentation of your assessment process, criteria, and decisions.

CSRD Implementation Timeline

Understanding the regulatory timeline is crucial for effective planning:

2023

Preparation Phase

Companies begin gap analysis, establish governance structures, and develop implementation plans.

2024

Initial Implementation

First wave of companies conduct double materiality assessments and begin data collection processes.

2025

First Reporting Period

Large EU companies publish their first CSRD-aligned sustainability statements for FY2024.

2026

Expansion Phase

Requirements extend to listed SMEs and non-EU companies with significant EU presence.
